ORDER IN COUNCIL making provision for the Constitution and Powers of a Legislative Council for Nigeria.—London, August 2, 1946(1)

At the Court at Buckingham Palace, the 2nd day of August, 1946

PRESENT: THE KING’S MOST EXCELLENT MAJESTY IN COUNCIL

Whereas by “The Nigeria (Legislative Council) Orders in Council, 1922 to 1941(2)”, provision is made for the constitution and powers of the Legislative Council of the Colony of Nigeria and the Southern Provinces of the Protectorate of Nigeria:

And whereas it is expedient to make other provision for the constitution and powers of a Legislative Council for Nigeria, including the said Colony, the whole of the said Protectorate and the Cameroons under British Mandate:

Now, therefore, His Majesty, by virtue and in exercise of the powers in that behalf by “The Foreign Jurisdiction Act, 1890(3)” and otherwise in His Majesty vested, is pleased, by and with the advice of his Privy Council, to order, and it is hereby ordered, as follows:—

Part I.—Preliminary

1.—(1) In this Order, unless the context otherwise requires:—

“the appointed day” means the day fixed under section 2 of this Order;

“the Cameroons” means the Cameroons under British Mandate;

“the Colony” means the Colony of Nigeria;

“the Eastern Provinces” means that portion of the Southern Provinces which for the time being comprises the Eastern Provinces thereof;

“the Executive Council” means the Executive Council of Nigeria as from time to time established;

“the existing Orders” means the Orders in Council mentioned in the Schedule to this Order;

“the Gazette” means the official Gazette of Nigeria;

“the Governor” means the Governor and Commander-in-Chief of Nigeria and includes the Officer for the time being Administering the Government and, to the extent to which a Deputy for the Governor is authorised to act, that Deputy;

*272

“the Governor in Council” means the Governor acting with the advice of the Executive Council, but not necessarily in accordance with that advice nor necessarily in such Council assembled;

“House” means the House of Chiefs and a House of Assembly;

“House of Assembly” means a House of Assembly established by this Order;

“House of Chiefs” means the House of Chiefs established by this Order;

“the Legislative Council” means the Legislative Council established by this Order;

“Meeting” means any sitting or sittings of the Legislative Council commencing when the Council first meets after being summoned at any time and terminating when the Council is adjourned sine die or at the conclusion of a session without adjournment;

“Native Authority”, “Native Advisory Council” and “Native Court” mean respectively a native authority, native advisory council and a native court constituted under any law for the time being in force in Nigeria;

“Nigeria” means the Colony and Protectorate and the Cameroons;

“the Northern Provinces” means that portion of the Protectorate which for the time being comprises the Northern Provinces thereof together with that portion of the Cameroons which, for the time being, is to be administered with the Northern Provinces of the Protectorate;

“the Protectorate” means the Protectorate of Nigeria, and for the purposes of this Order, reference to the Protectorate shall include the Cameroons;

“the Public Seal” means the Public Seal of Nigeria;

“the Supreme Court” means the Supreme Court of Nigeria;

“Region” means the area, for the time being, of the Northern or the Western or the Eastern Provinces;

“Secretary of State” means one of His Majesty’s Principal Secretaries of State;

“Session” means the meetings of the Legislative Council commencing when the Council first meets after being constituted under this Order, or after its prorogation or dissolution at any time, and terminating when the Council is prorogued or is dissolved without having been prorogued;

*273

“the Southern Provinces” means that portion of the Protectorate which is, for the time being, not included in the Northern Provinces, together with that portion of the Cameroons which is, for the time being, administered with the Southern Provinces;

“the Western Provinces” means that portion of the Southern Provinces which for the time being comprises the Western Provinces thereof.

(2) For the purposes of this Order

(a) any reference to any public officer by the term designating his office means the officer for the time being lawfully discharging the functions of that office, and

(b) a person shall not be deemed to hold an office of emolument under the Crown by reason only that he

(i) is a member of the Executive Council; or

(ii) is a Chief, a native authority or a member of a native authority, or of a native court or of a native advisory council; or

(iii) is in receipt of a pension or other like allowance in respect of service under the Crown.

(3) If it shall be declared by any law for the time being in force in Nigeria that an office shall not be an office of emolument under the Crown for all or any of the purposes of this Order, this Order shall have effect accordingly as if such law were enacted in this Order.

(4) “The Interpretation Act, 1889(4)”, shall apply to the interpretation of this Order as it applies to the interpretation of an Act of Parliament.

2. This Order may be cited as “The Nigeria (Legislative Council) Order in Council, 1946”, and shall come into operation on a date to be fixed by the Governor by Proclamation in the Gazette(5):

Provided that the Governor may, from time to time, by notice in the Gazette, bring into operation any of the provisions of this Order specified in such notice before the date so fixed by Proclamation.

3.—(1) The existing Orders are hereby revoked.

(2) The continued operation of any law in force in Nigeria immediately before the appointed day shall not be affected by reason only of the revocation of the said Orders.

*274

Part II.—The Legislative Council

4. The Legislative Council as constituted under the existing Orders immediately before the appointed day shall cease to exist and there shall be constituted a Legislative Council in and for Nigeria, in accordance with the provisions of this Order.

5. The Legislative Council shall consist of the Governor as President, 13 ex-officio members, 3 nominated official members, 24 nominated unofficial members and 4 elected members.

6. The ex-officio Members shall be—

  • The Chief Secretary to the Government;
  • The Chief Commissioners of the three Regions;
  • The Attorney-General;
  • The Financial Secretary
  • The Director of Medical Services;
  • The Development Secretary;
  • The Director of Education;
  • The Director of Agriculture;
  • The Director of Public Works;
  • The Commissioner of Labour;
  • The Commissioner of the Colony.

7. The Nominated Official Members shall be 3 Residents, one from each region, appointed by the Governor by Instrument under the Public Seal for each meeting of the Legislative Council.

8.—(1) The nominated unofficial members shall be—

(a) 4 members, being members of the House of Chiefs appointed by that House;

(b) 5 members, being unofficial members of the Northern House of Assembly, appointed by the unofficial members of that House;

(c) 2 members, being Chiefs appointed by the Governor from those Chiefs who are members of the Western House of Assembly;

(d) 4 members, being unofficial members of the Western House of Assembly, appointed by the unofficial members of that House;

(e) 5 members, being unofficial members of the Eastern House of Assembly, appointed by the unofficial members of that House;

(f) A member for the Colony appointed by the Governor after consultation with the Native Authorities in the Colony;

*275

(g) 3 members appointed by the Governor to represent interests or communities which, in his opinion, are not otherwise adequately represented.

(2) Appointments by the Governor shall be made by Instrument under the Public Seal and appointments by Houses shall be made by resolution of the unofficial members of the appropriate House who alone shall vote on such resolution.

(3) The Governor shall without delay report to His Majesty, through a Secretary of State, every appointment made by him of any person as a nominated member; and every person so appointed shall hold his seat on the Legislative Council during His Majesty’s pleasure, and, subject thereto, for the period provided by this Order.

9. The elected members shall be 3 members for Lagos and one member for Calabar, elected as provided in this Order.

10.—(l) Subject to the provisions of this Order, a person appointed as a nominated unofficial member shall vacate his seat at the expiration of 3 years from the date of his appointment but, if qualified, shall be eligible for re-appointment.

(2) If any person is appointed a temporary nominated unofficial member of the Legislative Council in accordance with the provisions of section 16 of this Order and such temporary appointment is immediately followed by his definitive appointment as a nominated unofficial member the said period of 3 years shall, unless the Instrument appointing him definitively otherwise provides, be reckoned from the date of his temporary appointment.

11. Subject to the provisions of this Order, a person elected as an elected member shall vacate his seat at the expiration of 3 years from the date of his election, but, if qualified, shall be eligible for re-election:

Provided that, whenever the seat of an elected member becomes vacant under the provisions of sub-section (1) of section 14 of this Order, any person elected to fill the vacancy thereby caused shall vacate his seat at the expiration of the remainder of the term of office of the person in whose place he is elected.

12. Any male person who, being otherwise qualified in accordance with the provisions of this Order:—

(a) is a British subject or a British protected person of the age of 21 years or upwards; and

(b) in the case of an elected member, is qualified to be registered as an elector for the area concerned;

*276

shall be qualified to be a nominated unofficial member or an elected member of the Legislative Council, and no other person shall be qualified to be appointed or elected thereto or, having been so appointed or elected, shall sit or vote in the Legislative Council.

13. No person shall be capable of being a nominated unofficial member or elected member of the Legislative Council or, having been appointed or elected thereto, shall sit or vote in the Legislative Council, who at the time of appointment or election—

(1) is, by virtue of his own act, under any acknowledgment of allegiance, obedience, or adherence to a foreign Power or State; or

(2) is an undischarged bankrupt, having been declared a bankrupt under any law in force in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction; or

(3) has, in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction, been sentenced to death or to imprisonment (by whatever name called) for a term exceeding 6 months, and has not either suffered the punishment to which he was sentenced or such other punishment as may by competent authority have been substituted therefor or received a free pardon; or

(4) is disqualified from practising as a legal or medical practitioner in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction by the order of any competent authority; or

(5) holds an office of emolument under the Crown; or

(6) is a party to any subsisting contract with the Government of Nigeria for and on account of the public service and—

(a) in the case of a member appointed by the Governor, has not disclosed to the Governor the nature of such contract and his interest therein; or

(b) in the case of a member appointed by a House, has not disclosed to -that House the nature of such contract and his interest therein; or

(c) in the case of an elected member has not published within one month before the day of election, in the Gazette and

*277

in some newspaper circulating in the area for which he is a candidate, a notice setting out the nature of such contract and his interest therein; or

(7) is, under any law in force in Nigeria, found or declared to be of unsound mind or adjudged to be a lunatic; or

(8) has within 5 years before the day of election received any relief from public funds, except such special relief as may be declared by any law for the time being in force in Nigeria not to be relief for the purposes of this paragraph.

14.—(1) The seat of a nominated unofficial member or elected member of the Legislative Council shall become vacant—

(a) upon his death; or

(b) if he shall, without the leave of the Governor previously obtained, be absent from the sessions of the Legislative Council or from Nigeria for more than 12 consecutive months or be absent from the sittings of the Legislative Council for a period of one calendar month during any session of the Legislative Council; or

(c) if he shall take any oath, or make any declaration or acknowledgment, of allegiance, obedience or adherence to any foreign Power or State; or shall do, concur in or adopt any act done with the intention that he shall become a subject or citizen of any foreign Power or State; or

(d) if he shall be declared a bankrupt under any law in force in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction; or

(e) if in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction he shall be sentenced to death or to imprisonment (by whatever name called) for a term exceeding 6 months; or

(f) if he shall become disqualified from practising as a legal or medical practitioner in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction by the order of any competent authority; or

(g) if, without the prior consent of the Governor, he shall become a party to any contract with the Government of Nigeria for and on account of the public service; or

*278

(h) if, under any law in force in Nigeria, he shall be found or declared to be of unsound mind or adjudged to be a lunatic; or

(i) if he shall become disqualified for membership of the Legislative Council under any law for the time being in force in Nigeria relating to offences connected with the election of members; or

(j) if he shall, by writing under his hand addressed to the Governor, resign his seat in the Legislative Council; or

(k) if, being elected member, he shall be appointed to any office of emolument under the Crown; or

(l) if, being a nominated unofficial member, he shall be appointed permanently to any office of emolument under the Crown; or

(m) if he shall otherwise cease to possess qualification for appointment or election, as the case may be, under the provisions of this Order.

(2) If any nominated unofficial member shall be appointed temporarily to any office of emolument under the Crown or shall be appointed to act in any such office, he shall not sit or vote in the Legislative Council so long as he continues to hold or to act in that office.

(3) The Governor may, by Instrument under the Public Seal, declare any nominated unofficial member to be incapable of discharging his functions as a member of the Legislative Council, and thereupon such member shall not sit or vote in the Council until he is declared, in manner aforesaid, again to be capable of discharging his said functions.

(4) The Governor may, by Instrument under the Public Seal, suspend any member appointed by him from the exercise of his functions as a member of the Legislative Council. Every such suspension shall forthwith be reported by the Governor to His Majesty through a Secretary of State and shall remain in force unless and until it shall be removed by the Governor by Instrument under the Public Seal or by His Majesty through a Secretary of State or the person suspended ceases to be a member of the Legislative Council.

(5) Whenever the seat of a member becomes vacant under sub-section (1) of this section, the vacancy shall forthwith be reported to the Governor in writing by the Clerk of the Legislative Council.

*279

15.—(1) All questions which may arise as to the right of any person to be or remain a nominated unofficial member shall be referred to and decided by the Governor in Council.

(2) All questions which may arise as to the right of any person to be or remain an elected member shall be referred to and decided by the Supreme Court in accordance with the provisions of any law for the time being in force in Nigeria.

16.—(1) Whenever there shall be a vacancy in the number of persons sitting as ex-officio or nominated members of the Council, by reason of the fact that:

(a) an official member is, as Governor’s Deputy, President of the Legislative Council; or

(b) one person is lawfully discharging the functions of more than one of the offices referred to in section 6 of this Order; or

(c) no person is lawfully discharging the functions of any such office; or

(d) a nominated official member is lawfully discharging the functions of any such office; or

(e) any such office has ceased to exist; or

(f) a member appointed by the Governor is suspended from the exercise of his functions as a member; or

(g) a nominated unofficial member is unable to sit or vote in the Legislative Council, in consequence of a declaration by the Governor, as provided in this Order, that he is incapable of discharging his functions as a member; or

(h) a member is absent from Nigeria; or

(i) a nominated unofficial member is temporarily appointed to an office of emolument under the Crown in Nigeria,

a person may be appointed as a temporary member for the period of such vacancy.

(2) If the vacancy is in the number of ex-officio or nominated official members, the person appointed shall be a person holding office of emolument under the Crown and shall hold his seat in the Legislative Council during His Majesty’s pleasure, and such temporary appointment shall be made by the Governor by Instrument under the Public Seal: and if the vacancy is in the number of nominated unofficial members the person appointed shall be a person possessing the same qualifications as the member to whose seat he is being so temporarily appointed and such temporary appointment shall be made by

*280

the Governor by Instrument under the Public Seal or by resolution of the appropriate House, as the case may require.

(3) The period of any such vacancy shall terminate when the circumstances giving rise to the vacancy shall cease to exist.

(4) Every person so temporarily appointed shall, as long as his appointment shall subsist, be to all intents and purposes—

(a) in the case of a vacancy in the number of persons sitting as ex-officio or nominated official members, a nominated official member; and

(b) in the case of a vacancy in the number of persons sitting as nominated unofficial members, a nominated unofficial member,

and the provisions of sub-section (1) of section 14 and sub-section (1) of section 15 of this Order. shall apply accordingly:

Provided that in the case of a vacancy in the number of persons sitting as ex-officio members of the Legislative Council, the person so appointed shall be deemed for the purposes of section 6 of this Order to be an ex-officio member.

17. The Governor, if present, or, in the absence of the Governor, such member of the Legislative Council as the Governor may from time to time generally or specially appoint, or in default of such appointment, or in the absence of the member so appointed, the member present who stands first in the order of precedence, shall preside at the meetings of the Legislative Council.

18.—(1) After the Governor, members of the Legislative Council shall take precedence as His Majesty may specially assign, and, in default thereof, as follows:—

First, the ex-officio members in the order in which they are mentioned in section 6 of this Order;

Secondly, the nominated official members in such order as the Governor may direct;

Thirdly, the nominated unofficial members and elected members according to the length of time during which they have continuously been members of the Legislative Council, members appointed or elected at the same time taking precedence among themselves in such order as the Governor may direct.

(2) For the purposes of this section:—

(a) when the Legislative Council is dissolved, nominated unofficial members appointed to fill vacancies caused thereby shall be deemed to have been appointed, and members elected at the ensuing elections shall be deemed to have been elected

*281

by virtue of those elections, on the date on which the report of the first successful candidate is made by a returning officer to the Governor; and

(b) Sub-paragraph (a) of this sub-section shall apply to the members appointed or elected to the Legislative Council as first constituted under this Order as if such elections or appointments were consequent upon a dissolution of the Legislative Council; and

(c) in ascertaining the period during which a person has continuously been a nominated unofficial member or elected member of the Legislative Council, no account shall be taken of any interval between the vacation by that person of his seat in the Legislative Council in consequence of the expiration of his tenure of office or a dissolution of the Legislative Council and the date of his appointment or election to fill a vacancy in the Legislative Council caused by that expiration of tenure or that dissolution; and

(d) if any person, having been, for any period immediately before the appointed day, an unofficial member of the Legislative Council constituted under the existing Orders, becomes an unofficial member of the Legislative Council as first constituted under this Order, he shall be deemed to have been a member of the Legislative Council so constituted under this Order for that period; and no account shall be taken of any interval between the day preceding the appointed day and the date upon which he is first so appointed or elected a member of the Legislative Council or of any interval in his membership of the Legislative Council constituted under the existing Orders between the expiration of his tenure of office and his appointment or election to fill the vacancy thereby caused.

19.—(1) Whenever the seat of a nominated unofficial member becomes vacant, the vacancy shall be filled by appointment by the Governor by Instrument under the Public Seal or by the appropriate House, as the case may require.

(2) Whenever the seat of an elected member becomes vacant a fresh election shall be held in the appropriate area in accordance with the provisions of this Order.

20.—(1) The first elections and appointments of members to the Legislative Council constituted under this Order shall be held and made not later than such date, within 3 ,months after the appointed day, as the Governor shall by notice in the Gazette direct.

*282

(2) The first session of the Legislative Council shall commence within 6 months after the appointed day.

Part III.—Legislation and Procedure of Legislature

21. Subject to the provisions of this Order, it shall be lawful for the Governor, with the advice and consent of the Legislative Council, to make laws for the peace, order and good government of Nigeria.

22. Subject to the provisions of this Order and of the Standing Rules and Orders of the Legislative Council, any member may introduce any Bill or propose any motion for debate in, or may present any petition to, the Legislative Council, and the same shall be debated and disposed of according to the Standing Rules and Orders:

Provided that, except by the direction or with the sanction or recommendation of the Governor signified thereto, the Council shall not proceed upon any Bill, amendment, motion or petition which, in the opinion of the Governor or in that of the presiding member, would dispose of or charge any public revenue or public funds of Nigeria, or revoke or alter any disposition thereof or charge thereon, or impose, alter or repeal any rate, tax or duty.

23. Save as otherwise provided in this Order, all questions proposed for decision in the Legislative Council shall be determined by a majority of the votes of the members present and voting. The Governor shall not have an original vote, but, if upon any question the votes shall be equally divided, he shall have a casting vote. In the absence of the Governor, the presiding member shall have an original vote and also, if upon any question the votes shall be equally divided, a casting vote.

24. The Legislative Council shall not be disqualified for the transaction of business by reason of any vacancy among the members; but no business except that of adjournment shall be transacted if objection is taken by any member present that there are less than 12 members present besides the President or other member presiding.

25. Subject to the provisions of this Order, the Governor and the Legislative Council shall, in the transaction of business and the passing of laws, conform as nearly as may be to the directions contained in any Instructions under His Majesty’s

*283

Sign Manual and Signet which may from time to time be addressed to the Governor in that behalf.

26.—(1) If the Governor shall consider that it is expedient in the interests of public order, public faith or good government (which expressions shall, without prejudice to their generality, include the responsibility of Nigeria as a component part of the British Empire, and all matters pertaining to the creation or abolition of any public office or to the appointment, salary or other conditions of service of any public officer or officers) that any Bill introduced, or any motion proposed for decision in -the Legislative Council should have effect, then, if the Legislative Council fail to pass such a Bill or motion within such time and in such form as the Governor may think reasonable and expedient, the Governor, at any time in his discretion, may, notwithstanding any provisions of this Order or of any Standing Rules and Orders of the Legislative Council, declare that such Bill or motion shall have effect as if it had been passed by the Legislative Council, either in the form in which it was so introduced or proposed or with such amendments as the Governor shall think fit which have been moved or proposed in the Legislative Council or in any Committee thereof; and thereupon the said Bill or motion shall have effect as if it had been so passed, and, in the case of any such Bill, the provisions of this Order relating to assent to Bills and disallowance of laws shall apply accordingly.

(2) The Governor shall forthwith report to a Secretary of State every case in which he shall make any such declaration and the reasons therefor.

(3) If any member of the Legislative Council objects to any declaration made under this section, he may, within 7 days of the making thereof, submit to the Governor a statement in writing of his reasons for so objecting, and a copy of such statement shall, if furnished by such a member, be forwarded by the Governor as soon as practicable to a Secretary of State.

(4) Any such declaration relating to a motion may be revoked by a Secretary of State, and the Governor shall notify such revocation in the Gazette; and from the date of such notification any motion which shall have had effect by virtue of the declaration revoked shall cease to have effect; and the provisions of sub-section (2) of section 38 of “The Interpretation Act, 1889”, shall apply to such revocation as they apply to the repeal of an Act of Parliament.

*284

27.—(1) No Bill shall become a law until either the Governor shall have assented thereto in His Majesty’s name and on His Majesty’s behalf and shall have signed the same in token of such assent, or His Majesty shall have given his assent thereto through a Secretary of State.

(2) When a Bill is presented to the Governor for his assent, he shall, according to his discretion, but, subject to the provisions of any Instructions addressed to him under His Majesty’s Sign Manual and Signet or through a Secretary of State, declare that he assents, or refuses his assent, thereto, or that he reserves the Bill for the signification of His Majesty’s pleasure:

Provided that the Governor shall reserve for the signification of His Majesty’s pleasure any Bill by which any provision of this Order is repealed or amended or which is in any way repugnant to or inconsistent with the provisions of this Order, unless he shall have been authorised by a Secretary of State to assent thereto.

(3) A law assented to by the Governor shall be published in the Gazette and shall come into operation on the date of such publication, or, if it shall be enacted, either in the law or in some other enactment (including any enactment in force on the appointed day), that it shall come into operation on some other date, on that date.

(4) A Bill reserved for the signification of His Majesty’s pleasure shall become a law so soon as His Majesty shall have given his assent thereto, through a Secretary of State, and the Governor shall have signified such assent by Proclamation in the Gazette. Every such law shall come into operation on the date of such Proclamation or, if it shall be enacted, either in the law or in some other enactment (including any enactment in force on the appointed day), that it shall come into operation on some other date, on that date.

28.—(l) Any law to which the Governor shall have given his assent may be disallowed by His Majesty through a Secretary of State.

(2) Whenever any law has been disallowed by His Majesty, the Governor shall cause notice of such disallowance to be published in the Gazette.

(3) Every law so disallowed shall cease to have effect as soon as notice of such disallowance shall be published as aforesaid; and thereupon any enactment repealed or amended by or in pursuance of the law disallowed shall have effect as if such law had

*285

not been made. Subject as aforesaid the provisions of sub-section (2) of section 38 of “The Interpretation Act, 1889”, shall apply to such disallowance as they apply to the repeal of an Act of Parliament.

29.—(1) The Sessions of the Legislative Council and meetings thereof during a session and sittings thereof during a meeting shall be held at such times and places as the Governor shall from time to time by Proclamation or notice appoint.

(2) There shall be a Session of the Council once at least in every year, so that a period of 12 months shall not intervene between the last sitting in one session and the first sitting in the next session.

30.—(1) Subject to the provisions of this Order and of any Instructions under His Majesty’s Sign Manual and Signet, the Legislative Council may from time to time make, amend and revoke Standing Rules and Orders for the regulation and orderly conduct of their proceedings and the despatch of business, to provide for the giving of notice of the provisions of Bills and for the presentation thereof to the Governor for assent:

Provided that no such Rules or Orders shall have effect unless and until they shall have been approved by the Governor.

(2) Until other provision is made under this section, the Standing Rules and Orders of the Legislative Council constituted under the existing Orders, as in force immediately before the appointed day, shall, with the necessary modifications and adaptations, be the Standing Rules and Orders of the Legislative Council constituted under this Order; and the said Standing Rules and Orders may be amended or revoked by Standing Rules and Orders made under the preceding sub-section.

31.—(1) The Governor may at any time, by Proclamation, prorogue or dissolve the Legislative Council.

(2) On dissolution all members shall vacate their seats; and the Legislative Council shall be reconstituted by appointment and election in accordance with the provisions of this Order at such time, within 3 months of the dissolution, as the Governor shall by Proclamation appoint.

32. Save for the purpose of enabling this section to be complied with, no member of the Legislative Council shall sit or

*286

vote therein until he shall have taken and subscribed the following oath before the Legislative Council:—

“I, A.B., do swear that I will be faithful and bear true allegiance to His Majesty King George VI, his heirs and successors according to law. So help me God”:

Provided that any person, so authorised by any law for the time being in force in Nigeria, may make an affirmation in like terms or take the like oath in any form or manner authorised by any such law as aforesaid.

Part IV.—The House of Chiefs and the Houses of Assembly

33.—(1) There shall be established in and for the Northern Provinces a Northern Regional Council consisting of the House of Chiefs and a House of Assembly to be called the Northern House of Assembly.

(2) The House of Chiefs shall consist of the following members:

(a) The Chief Commissioner for the Northern Provinces as President;

(b) All first class Chiefs exercising their functions as such within the Northern Provinces and qualified under this Order to sit in the House; and

(c) Not less than 10 Members selected from their own number by second class Chiefs, exercising their functions as within the Northern Provinces. The number of such Chiefs, the manner of their selection and the tenure of their office shall be such as the Governor may, from time to time, direct in writing.

(3) Where a first class Chief is, under the provisions of this Order, disqualified for membership of the House of Chiefs, or where the office of any first class Chief is temporarily vacant, the person being otherwise qualified under the provisions of this Order, who is lawfully performing the functions of such Chief may, with the approval of the Governor, sit and vote as a member of the House of Chiefs in the seat of the first class Chief so disqualified or as a member in respect of the office so temporarily vacant, as the case may be.

(4) The Northern House of Assembly shall consist of the following members:—

(a) 19 official members, who shall be—

The Senior Resident, Northern Provinces, as President;

*287

12 Residents of the Northern Provinces (the Secretary,Northern Provinces, being regarded as a Resident);

The Secretary (Finance), Northern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Medical Services, Northern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Education, Northern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Agriculture, Northern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Public Works, Northern Provinces;

The Senior Crown Counsel, Northern Provinces; and

(b) Not less than 20 nor more than 24 unofficial members who shall be:—

(i) Such number of members, not being less than 14 nor more than 18 and not being members of the House of Chiefs (who shall be called Provincial members), selected as hereinafter provided, as the Governor may from time to time direct in writing;

(ii) 6 members appointed by the Governor to represent interests and communities which, in his opinion, are not otherwise adequately represented.

34.—(1) There shall be established in and for the Western Provinces a House of Assembly, to be called the Western House of Assembly.

,(2) The Western House of Assembly shall consist of the following members:—

(a) 14 official members, who shall be—

The Chief Commissioner for the Western Provinces as President;

7 Residents of the Western Provinces (the Secretary, Western Provinces, being regarded as a Resident);

The Secretary (Finance), Western Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Medical Services, Western Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Education, Western Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Agriculture, Western Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Public Works, Western Provinces;

The Senior Crown Counsel, Western Provinces; and

(b) Not less than 15 nor more than 19 unofficial members who shall be:—

(i) 3 Head Chiefs of the Western Provinces to be appointed by the Governor after consultation with the Head Chiefs of the Western Provinces;

*288

(ii) Such number of members, not being less than 7 nor more than 11 (who shall be called Provincial members), selected as hereinafter provided, as the Governor may from time to time direct in writing;

(iii) 5 members appointed by the Governor to represent interests or communities which, in his opinion, are not otherwise adequately represented.

35.—(1) There shall be established in and for the Eastern Provinces a House of Assembly, to be called the Eastern House of Assembly.

(2) The Eastern House of Assembly shall consist of the following members:—

(a) 14 official members, who shall be—

The Chief Commissioner for the Eastern Provinces as President;

7 Residents of the Eastern Provinces (the Secretary, Eastern Provinces, being regarded as a Resident);

The Secretary (Finance), Eastern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Medical Services, Eastern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Education, Eastern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Agriculture, Eastern Provinces;

The Deputy Director of Public Works, Eastern Provinces:

The Senior Crown Counsel, Eastern Provinces; and

(b) Not less than 15 nor more than 18 unofficial members who shall be:—

(i) Such number of members, not being less than 10 nor more than 13 (who shall be called Provincial Members), selected as hereinafter provided, as the Governor may from time to time direct in writing;

(ii) 5 members appointed by the Governor to represent interests or communities which, in his opinion, are not otherwise adequately represented.

36. The Governor may, from time to time, declare in writing the Residents who shall be members of each House of Assembly and may describe any Resident or Residents by name or by office or by reference to the functions performed by him or them or in any other manner which the Governor may think fit.

37. The Provincial members shall be selected by such Native Authorities in the Northern, Western or Eastern Provinces (as the case may be) from among their own members or, where a Native Authority consists of a sole Chief or other

*289

person, from among the members of his Advisory Council, and in such manner, as the Governor may from time to time direct in writing.

38.—(1) Any male person, who, being otherwise qualified in accordance with the provisions of this Order,

(a) is a British subject or a British protected person of the age of 21 years or upwards; and

(b) is a person of African descent shall be qualified to be a member of the House of Chiefs or an unofficial member of a House of Assembly, as the case may be, and no other person shall be qualified to be a member of the House of Chiefs or an unofficial member of any such House. or having been so selected or appointed shall sit or vote in any such House:

Provided that the provisions of this section shall not apply to the President of the House of Chiefs.

(2) For the purposes of this section “a person of African descent” means a person whose parents or one of whose parents were members or was a member of a tribe or tribes indigenous to some part of Africa.

39. Subject to the provisions of this Order, a person selected or appointed an unofficial member of a House of Assembly shall hold his seat for a period of 3 years from the date of his selection or appointment, but, if qualified, shall be eligible to be again selected or appointed for a further period or periods.

40. No Chief shall be capable of being a member of the House of Chiefs and no person shall be capable of being appointed or selected an unofficial member of a House of Assembly or being a first class Chief or having been appointed or selected a member of a House shall sit or vote therein who:

(1) is, by virtue of his own act, under any acknowledgment of allegiance, obedience or adherence to a foreign Power or State; or

(2) is an undischarged bankrupt, having been declared a bankrupt under any law in force in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction; or

(3) has, in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in

*290

which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction, been sentenced to death or to imprisonment (by whatever name called) for a term exceeding 6 months. and has not either suffered the punishment to which he was sentenced. or such other punishment as may by competent authority have been substituted therefor, or received a free pardon; or

(4) is disqualified from practising as a legal or medical practitioner in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction by the order of any competent authority; or

(5) holds an office of emolument under the Crown; or

(6) is a party to any subsisting contract with the Government of Nigeria for and on account of the public service and—

(a) in the case of a first class Chief or a member appointed by the Governor, has not disclosed to the Governor the nature of such contract and his interest therein; or

(b) in the case of any other member, has not disclosed to the body selecting him the nature of such contract and his interest therein; or

(7) is, under any law in force in Nigeria, found or declared to be of unsound mind or adjudged to be a lunatic.

41.—(1) The seat of a Chief, other than a first class Chief, in the House of Chiefs and the seat of an unofficial member of a House of Assembly shall become vacant:

(a) upon his death; or

(b) if he shall, without the leave of the President of the House previously obtained, be absent from two consecutive meetings of the House, or if he shall be absent from the Northern, Western or Eastern Provinces, as the case may be, for a period of 12 consecutive months; or

(c) if he shall take any oath, or make any declaration or acknowledgment, of allegiance, obedience or adherence to any foreign Power or State; or shall do, concur in or adopt any act done with the intention that he shall become a subject or citizen of any foreign Power or State; or

(d) if he shall be declared a bankrupt under any law in force in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction; or

(e) if in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in

*291

which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction he shall be sentenced to death, or to imprisonment (by whatever name called) for a term exceeding 6 months; or

(f) if he shall become disqualified from practising as a legal or medical practitioner in any part of His Majesty’s dominions or in any territory under His Majesty’s protection or in any territory in which His Majesty has from time to time jurisdiction by the order of any competent authority; or

(g) if he shall become a party to any contract with the Government of Nigeria for and on account of the public service and has not—

(i) in the case of a member appointed by the Governor, obtained the prior consent of the Governor; or

(ii) in the case of any other member, obtained the prior consent of the body selecting him; or

(h) if, under any law in force in Nigeria, he shall be found or declared to be of unsound mind or adjudged to be a lunatic; or

(i) if he shall, by writing under his hand directed to the President of House concerned, resign his seat in that House; or

(j) if he shall be appointed permanently to any office of emolument under the Crown; or

(k) if he shall otherwise cease to possess qualification for the membership for or to which he was selected or appointed.

(2) If any Chief, being a member of the House of Chiefs, or any unofficial member of a House of Assembly shall be appointed temporarily to any office of emolument under the Crown or shall be appointed to act in any such office, he shall not sit or vote in the House so long as he continues to hold or to act in that post.

(3) The Governor may, by Instrument under the Public Seal, declare any Chief, being a member of the House of Chiefs, or any unofficial member of a House of Assembly to be incapable of discharging his functions as a member of that House, and thereupon such member shall not sit or vote in that House until he is declared, in manner aforesaid, again to be capable of discharging his said functions.

42. Whenever upon any special occasion the President of a House desires that that House shall have the advice of any person in Nigeria upon any matter before it, he may summon for such special occasion any such person as an extraordinary

*292

member of that House. An extraordinary member shall not, have the right to vote on any matter before that House.

43.—(l) The President of the House of Chiefs shall take precedence before the other members, who shall take precedence among themselves as the Governor may specifically assign, and, in default thereof, in order of their precedence as Chiefs.

(2) The President of a House of Assembly shall take precedence before the other members, who shall take precedence among themselves as the Governor may specifically assign, and, in default thereof, as follows:—

First, the official members in the Order in which they are mentioned in section 33, or section 34, or section 35 of this Order, as the case may be, Residents taking precedence among themselves according to their seniority in the Administrative Service of Nigeria;

Secondly, the unofficial members according to the length of time during which they have continuously been members of the House concerned, members appointed or selected at the same time taking precedence among themselves in such order as the Governor may assign:

Provided that, if any unofficial member is appointed or selected immediately on the termination of his term of office to fill the vacancy caused by such termination, in determining the period for which he has been continuously a member of the House any interval between such termination and appointment or selection shall be disregarded.

44.—(1) The President of a House may, by writing under his hand, suspend any member of such House from the exercise of his functions as a member thereof.

(2) Every such suspension shall forthwith be reported to the Governor and shall remain in force unless and until it shall be removed by the Governor by an Instrument under the Public Seal or the person suspended ceases to be a member of that House.

45. All questions which may arise as to the right of any person to be or remain a member of a House shall be referred to and decided by the Governor in Council.

46. Save as otherwise provided in this Order, all questions proposed for decision in a House shall be determined by the majority of the votes of the members present and voting. The President of a House or other member presiding, as the case

*293

may be, shall have an original vote and also, if upon any question the votes shall be equally divided, a casting vote.

47. In the absence of the President of a House, such member of that House as the President may from time to time appoint, or in default of such appointment, or in the absence of the member so appointed, the member present who stands first in the order of precedence, shall preside at the meetings of the House.

48. A House shall not be disqualified for the transaction of business by reason of any vacancy among the members; but no business, except that of adjournment, shall be transacted if objection is taken by any member present that there are, besides the President or other Presiding member, less than the following number of members present:—

(a) in the case of the House of Chiefs, 12 members;

(b) in the case of the Northern House of Assembly, 10 members;

(c) in the case of the Western House of Assembly, 9 members;

(d) in the case of the Eastern House of Assembly, 9 members;

49.—(1) Subject to the provisions of this Order, a House may, from time to time, make, amend and revoke Standing Rules and Orders for the regulation and orderly conduct of proceedings and the despatch of business, and such Standing Rules and Orders may contain provision for the official language or languages of such House:

Provided that no such Rules or Orders shall have effect unless and until they shall have been approved by the President.

(2) In the case of the Houses of the Northern Regional Council, the official language or one of the official languages shall be Hausa.

50.—(1) The Governor may at any time by Instrument under the Public Seal prorogue or dissolve a House.

(2) On dissolution all members shall vacate their seats; and such House shall be reconstituted, in accordance with the provisions of this Order, at such time within 6 months of the dissolution as the Governor shall, by Instrument under the Public Seal, appoint.

Part V.—Functions of the House of Chiefs and Houses of Assembly

51. Subject to the provisions of sub-section (2) of this section, the functions of each House shall be:—

(a) to appoint members to the Legislative Council as provided in part II of this Order; and

(b) to exercise the powers in relation to the estimates of the region and in relation to Bills conferred upon the House by the provisions of this part of this Order; and

(c) to consider and, by resolution, advise on any question or matter referred to the House by the Governor and on any question or matter introduced by a member for the consideration of the House in accordance with the provisions of this Order; and

(d) to perform such other functions as may be prescribed by or under any law for the time being in force in Nigeria.

52.—(1) There shall be presented to each House of Assembly the annual estimates of expenditure for the region together with a statement of the estimated revenue to be apportioned or assigned to that region for that year and the House may, by resolution, make recommendations in respect of the said annual estimates of expenditure.

(2) The said annual estimates and the said statement for the Northern Provinces, after consideration by the Northern House of Assembly, together with the resolution of the Northern House of Assembly thereon, shall be presented to the House of Chiefs; and the House of Chiefs may exercise in respect of the said annual estimates powers similar to those of the Northern House of Assembly.

(3) The said annual estimates and the said statement for each region, after consideration by the Houses as hereinbefore provided, together with the resolutions of the Houses thereon, shall be submitted to the Governor for approval.

(4) The Governor may approve, with or without modification, or may reject the recommendations of any House, and, before giving his approval, may make such amendments in the said estimates and statement for any region as he may consider necessary.

(5) The annual estimates of expenditure for each region as approved by the Governor and the resolutions of each House on such estimates together with the statement of apportioned or

*295

assigned revenues for each region shall be laid on the table of the Legislative Council on the first day of the meeting of that Council during which the annual estimates for Nigeria are to be considered in connection with the annual Appropriation Bill and shall be included in the estimates for Nigeria in such manner as the Governor may direct.

(6) Supplementary estimates of expenditure for any region, as from time to time required, shall be presented to the House of Assembly for the region concerned and, in the case of the Northern Provinces, to the House of Chiefs; and the provisions of sub-sections (1), (2), (3) and (4) of this section shall apply to such estimates in the same manner as they apply to annual estimates of expenditure.

(7) Any supplementary estimates of expenditure, as approved by the Governor, and any resolution of any House thereon shall be laid upon the table of the Legislative Council as soon as practicable after approval.

(8) The Governor may, from time to time, direct in writing the amounts of the revenues of Nigeria to -be apportioned or assigned to each region and the particular heads, sub-heads or items of such revenues to be so apportioned or assigned and may from time to time in like manner specify the services and works to be included in regional estimates of expenditure.

(9) The financial year of each region shall coincide with the financial year for Nigeria.

(10) The provisions of sub-sections (1), (2), (3), (4) and (6) of this section, and, in so far as they relate to resolutions of the Houses, the provisions of sub-sections (5) and (7) thereof shall not come into operation until such date as -may be appointed by the Governor by Proclamation in the Gazette.

53.—(1) Subject to the provisions of this section, every Bill other than Appropriation Bills and Supplementary Appropriation Bills, to be introduced into the Legislative Council shall, prior to his introduction therein, be laid upon the table of each House and the House shall consider the Bill and may submit to the Governor such advice thereon as the House may, by resolution, decide:

Provided that—

(i) the failure of a House to consider or advise upon a Bill, or delay in so doing, shall not prevent the introduction thereof into the Legislative Council, and, if the Bill becomes law, shall not affect the validity thereof;

*296

(ii) it shall not be obligatory to lay upon the table of any House any Bill which is in terms inapplicable to the region in and for which that House is established.

(2) A resolution passed by a House under the provisions of sub-section (1) of this section shall be laid on the table of the Legislative Council before the Bill to which such resolution relates is read a first time.

(3) This section shall not apply to a Bill which, prior to its introduction into the Legislative Council, is certified in writing by the Governor to be of a purely formal nature or to be too urgent to permit of its consideration by the Houses.

(4) The provisions of this section shall not come into operation until such date as may be appointed by the Governor by Proclamation in the Gazette.

54. Subject ito the provisions of this Order and of the Standing Rules and Orders of the House, any member of a House may propose any motion for debate in, or present any petition to, the House, and the same shall be debated and disposed of according to the Standing Rules and Orders:

Provided that, except by the direction or with the sanction or recommendation of the President signified thereto, the House shall not proceed upon any motion or petition which in the opinion of the President, would, if approved by the Governor, dispose of or charge any public revenue or public fund of Nigeria or of the region concerned, or revoke or alter any disposition thereof or charge thereon, or impose, alter or repeal any rate, tax or duty.

55. Save for the purpose of enabling this section to be complied with, no member of a House shall sit or vote therein until he shall have taken before that House the oath or affirmation required by the Standing Rules and Orders of the House.

Part VI.—Miscellaneous

56.—1) Provision may be made by any law enacted under this Order regarding the election of elected members including (without prejudice to the generality of the foregoing power) the following matters, that is to say:—

(a) the qualifications of voters for election of such members;

(b) the registration of voters;

(c) the ascertainment of the qualification of voters and candidates;

*297

(d) the holding of elections of such members;

(e) offences in relation to such elections, including the trial and punishment thereof; and

(f) the disposal of election petitions, and the determination of questions otherwise arising regarding membership of the Legislative Council.

(2) Until other provision has been made as aforesaid,

(a) the Governor may by Proclamation make provision for the purposes aforesaid and may thereby add to, amend or revoke “The Nigeria (Legislative Council) (Registration of Electors) Regulations” and “The Nigeria (Legislative Council) (Registration of Electors) Regulations (Amendment) Ordinance, 1941 (Ordinance No. 29 of 1941)”; and

(b) subject to any such Proclamation, the said Regulations and Ordinance shall apply in relation to the matters aforesaid, references therein to the Legislative Council being construed as references to the Legislative Council constituted by this Order.

57.—(1) Any person who—

(a) having been appointed or elected a member of the Legislative Council, but not having been, at the time of such appointment or election, qualified to be so appointed or elected, shall sit or vote in the Legislative Council; or

(b) having been appointed or selected a member of a House, but not having been, at the time of such appointment or selection, qualified to be so appointed or selected, shall sit or vote in the House; or

(c) shall sit or vote in the Legislative Council, or in a House, after his seat therein has become vacant, or after he has become disqualified from sitting or voting therein;

knowing, or having reasonable grounds for knowing, that he was so disqualified, or that his seat has become vacant, as the case may be, shall be liable to a penalty of £20 for every day upon which he so sits or votes.

(2) The said penalty shall be recoverable by action in the Supreme Court at the suit of the Attorney-General.

58. The provision of sections 2, 3 and 4 of “The Colonial Laws Validity Act, 1865”([6]), shall have effect in relation to the Protectorate as they apply in relation to the Colony as if they were herein set out and in terms made applicable to the Protectorate.

*298

59.—(1) If any difficulty shall arise in bringing into operation any of the provisions of this Order or in giving effect to the purposes thereof, a Secretary of State may, by Order, make such provision as seems to him necessary or expedient for the purpose of removing the difficulty and may, by such Order, amend or add to any provision of this Order:

Provided that no Order under this section shall be made later than January 1, 1949.

(2) Any Order made under this section may be amended, added to or revoked by a further Order and may be given retrospective effect to a day not earlier than the date of this Order.

60.—(1) His Majesty hereby reserves to himself, his heirs and successors power, with the advice of his or their Privy Council, to revoke, add to or amend this Order, as to him or them shall seem fit.

(2) Nothing in this Order shall affect the power of His Majesty in Council to make laws from time to time for the peace, order and good government of Nigeria.
